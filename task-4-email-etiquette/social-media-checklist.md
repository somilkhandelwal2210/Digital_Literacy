Social media Do's and Don'ts for a responsible college student

5 Do's 

1. Start by searching your own name online. What shows up first? Recruiters notice the same results. Internship managers check too. Your digital trail begins there.

2. Privacy begins with a quick check of your account settings. What you let others see changes everything, even if only a little. On places such as Instagram or Snapchat, only those you pick will notice what appears. Elsewhere - take LinkedIn - it matters to remain seen, ready for new links. The way people find you online grows from choices made one at a time. Hiding nothing does less than choosing wisely when to step forward. Where you are decides who gets near, again and again.

3. Show your successes by sharing certifications on work-related networks. When you complete a coding challenge, mention it online instead of staying quiet. Finishing a project? Post what you built where others can see. Hackathons matter too - talk about them after the event ends.

4. Before you share something online, stop for a moment. Ten seconds can help - use them to check whether that story or message holds up. Truth matters when passing things along. A quick look might show it is false. Spreading wrong information starts small, often without meaning to. Give it a second thought instead. Waiting briefly keeps rumors from growing. What seems real at first glance may not be.

5. Picture every post lasting forever. A snap might vanish after a day, yet screens grab what's shown. How you treat others shows up in every word you type. Kindness sticks around long after you hit send. Your voice stays kind because traces remain beyond time.

5 Don'ts 

1. Losing your temper online? A message meant for a teacher, a peer, or someone at the front desk can shift when it hits the web. Shouting into open rooms changes how words are seen - what seems right then might sting more after.

2. Staying safe means keeping your current spot off social media. Instead of sharing right away, hold back photos till you’ve moved on. What matters is timing - post later, once you’re gone. Being somewhere now? Best not to say. Pictures can wait without losing their value. Safety wins when details stay hidden until it’s too late for risks.

3. Walk away from online bullies. Getting into fights online drains your time while making you look careless. A trail of angry replies sticks around longer than you think.

4. Wait. A second too soon, that clip of you dancing could land in the wrong inbox. Picture it popping up between study notes and deadlines. Maybe hold back. Sunsets and spins might clash with career moves on screen. One tap changes everything - suddenly jokes sit beside job plans. Laughter spreads quick when timing fails. Before sharing something bright, check who waits in the shadows. Silence can speak louder than reactions. Some things belong elsewhere. Hesitate just long enough

5. Using the same password everywhere? That’s risky. Picture logging into your university system with a code you also use on TikTok or Spotify. A breach in one spot opens every door. Hackers crawl through weak points until everything falls. Protect each account like it guards something different. One leak spreads faster than you think.
