UPI collect request scams affecting college students

Here lies the offense
Money vanishes when someone deceives you into approving a transfer by mistake. One common move involves fake requests for payment, making targets think they’re accepting cash - only to lose funds instead.

Who is usually targeted:
Young adults studying at universities across India often get caught in scams. Especially during exchanges of secondhand goods - think notebooks, computers - through web platforms or local chat circles tied to school life. Deals made through mobile message threads connected to dorm housing also carry risk. These situations open doors to dishonest players looking to take advantage.

How it typically happens (Step-by-step):
1. A bicycle sits listed by someone needing cash fast. Right away one person jumps at the chance to buy it - no back and forth, just quick agreement. Payment? Supposedly sent already through UPI they say. The seller checks their account. Nothing shows up. Turns out the payment was fake - a screenshot made to look real. Trust builds fast in these moments. That trust gets broken faster. Messages go unanswered after that. What seemed like luck turns into loss.
2. A request shows up on the phone, not a transfer. Someone pretending to be helpful asks for approval through a UPI Collect notice. A strange QR pops into view inside the app. It looks routine until it isn’t. Payment flows backward when tapped. The screen says it’s correct. Trust moves faster than caution here. Approval hands control away silently.
3. Faster talk rushes through the phone, a stranger reaching out to a student. Pressure builds fast, words packed tight without pause. A demand slips in - approve what comes next, type in your UPI PIN. Money, they say, will land only if those steps are followed
4. A young learner hits enter on their UPI code - thinking it's part of signing up, not knowing it hands away cash. Mistakes stack fast when details blur under pressure. One click drains what took weeks to save. Trust leaks out through small gaps like this.

Consequences:
Right away, cash vanishes from the student’s bank - no deposit made. Their profile disappears just as fast, wiped clean by the fraudster who also cuts off contact. When every dollar counts each month, losing funds hits hard, shaking confidence along with savings.
